## 0. How to write Ember marketing (golden rules)

### Winning formula

Every strong line should do three things:

1. **Nail the pain** — the real parent moment (known or unknown-unknown)  
2. **Sound like a UK parent** — plain English, warm, not clinical  
3. **Use a concrete example** — night lights, stacking cups, kitchen stools, nursery bags  

**Best-in-class example:**  
“Stop relatives guessing — and stop duplicate night lights.”

### Anti-patterns (ban in public copy)

| Don’t | Why | Do instead |
|-------|-----|------------|
| “Calm”, “worth buying”, “might be”, “tight shortlist” | AI / soft waffle | Concrete verb + specific thing |
| “AI”, “flows”, “engine”, “algorithm” | UK parents often wary of tech-speak | Imply smarts via outcome (“list from a photo”, “matched to their age”) |
| Naming competitors (Facebook Marketplace, Lovevery, Amazon…) | Pink-elephant + sour | Paint Ember’s upside; let the contrast hang unspoken |
| Pink elephants (“not spam”, “not a feed”, “not 40 tabs” as lead) | Plants the fear | Lead with the good; only contrast when needed, briefly |
| Underselling Free / overhyping Plus as “unlock” SaaS | Either weakens Free or sounds US growth-hack | **Free = good.** **Plus = great.** Same product family. |
| FOMO panic (“never miss out”) | Pushy | Positive aheadness: kitchen-stool story (see §1) |

### Free vs Plus mentality

- **Free is GOOD** — groundbreaking catalogue + Smart Marketplace; fine if you want to do everything yourself.  
- **Plus is GREAT** — Pip brings Ember to life: the product starts working *for* you, not only when you remember to open it.  
- Never imply Free is a demo. Never imply Plus is mandatory.

### How we talk about “smart” without saying AI

Parents should *feel* intelligence. Infer it, don’t badge it:

- “List something from a photo”  
- “Matched to what children this age actually need”  
- “Pip watches what’s next”  
- “A short list, already sorted”

## 1. How a parent should feel

| Feeling | What it means in practice |
|---------|---------------------------|
| **Ahead of the curve** | They hear about the useful thing *before* their friend group — not because they’re panicking, but because they didn’t know it existed. **Founding story:** the kitchen stool — toddler loved it; you text a friend to recommend one; she’s had hers for six months. You weren’t scrambling. You just felt behind. An unknown unknown. That’s why Ember exists. |
| **Empowered** | Clear choices: buy / borrow / bring back out / wait / pass on |
| **Relatable** | Sounds like a smart UK parent friend, not a clinic or a catalogue |
| **Relieved** | “Someone already did the hard thinking” |
| **Smart** | Good judgement, sharpened by a clever app — without tech-bro speak. The “how” shows up as useful magic (photo list, age match, Pip watching) rather than a lab coat. |

**Test line:** Would Conor think *“that gets it”* — or roll his eyes?

## 2. Positioning (one-liners)

### Ember (brand) — mission / aspiration

**Approved direction:**  
Ember helps you stay ahead of toddlerhood — knowing what matters now, what to get next, and what you can leave on the shelf — so you feel one step ahead without living on forums.

**Talking points:**

1. Unknown unknowns (kitchen stool) — hear about useful kit before the WhatsApp group catches you out.  
2. Less research spiral, more “that’s exactly where we are.”  
3. Know it / buy it / move it on — the full loop of family kit, not only shopping.

### Ember Free

The full stage catalogue and Smart Marketplace you can use forever — browse, save, gift-list, list and match — in your own time.

**Talking points:**

1. Forever is fine — Free is a real product if you prefer self-serve.  
2. Groundbreaking depth for £0: stage ideas + Marketplace, not a teaser.  
3. You stay in the driving seat; Ember’s there when you open it.

### Ember Plus (Pip)

Pip is when Ember starts working for you — watching what’s next, landing the short list, and tapping you when something local or seasonal or life-sized actually needs a decision.

**Talking points:**

1. Free is the map; Plus is Pip on the path with you.  
2. The catalogue comes to *you* (Pathway, seasons, moments) instead of you always hunting.  
3. Buy / gift / local / clear-out — covered without another evening lost to tabs.

## 3. Voice blend (approved)

Two flavours, used **subtly** and only when they earn their place:

### A) Parenting analogies

Homework, rabbit holes, bedtime scrolling, “already got one in the cupboard”, bring it back out, kitchen stool, WhatsApp recommendation after the fact.

### B) Light Pip / avian flavour

The **logo** does “robin.” Copy may use: watching what’s next, landing an answer, pathway, perch, nest, flight — never “Pip the robin” in customer-facing lines.

### Do

- Plain UK English  
- Specific examples (cups, nursery, Christmas list, night lights, stools)  
- Respect what they already own  
- Treat Free as GOOD; treat Plus as GREAT  

### Don’t

- Patronise (“baby’s first blocks”)  
- Panic FOMO — prefer positive aheadness  
- SaaS clichés: unlock, next level, optimise, must-have, essential, revolutionary, AI-powered  
- US spelling (**personalised**)  
- Licensed character IPs as preference examples unless legal-cleared  
- Pink elephants that plant the complaint we’re denying

## 4. Free vs Plus (tier rules)

| | **Ember Free** | **Ember Plus** |
|--|----------------|----------------|
| **Job** | Trustworthy stage map + Smart Marketplace | Pip brings the right bit of Ember to them |
| **Feel** | Capable, self-serve, complete | Relieved, ahead, helped |
| **Quality bar** | GOOD — keep forever | GREAT — Ember at full strength |
| **Never say** | “Just browsing” / “limited” / “basic listings” | That Free is pointless alone |
| **Always true** | Stage-personalised ideas; research-backed guidance; Smart Marketplace; saves/gift list | Pathway, Picks, Proximity, Seasons, Moments, Move-On (as shipped) |

**Pricing page rule:** Free bullets = a product you’d keep. Plus bullets = one line each: **what it is — why it helps**.

## 6. Competitive frames (implicit only)

**Rule:** Do not name competitors in parent-facing copy. Use Ember’s upside so the contrast is felt, not shouted.

| Internal foil (for writers) | Say this instead (public) |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------|
| Expensive stage boxes | Stage confidence that respects what you already own — buy only when it earns its place |
| Endless local scroll / chat groups | Smart Marketplace + Proximity: need-first matches, photo listing, age sense |
| Forum + shop-tab spirals | Know what matters this age in minutes — judgment included |
| Generic parenting content apps | UK parent reality: buy / borrow / bring back out / pass on |

---

## 7. Pricing page rules (for when we rewrite)

1. Hero: Free promise + bold Pip/Plus beat (aspiration without SaaS clichés).  
2. Free box: GOOD product — Discover depth, Smart Marketplace, gift list.  
3. Plus box: GREAT product — one specific line per Pip feature (what — why).  
4. Meet Pip: parenting metaphor OK; end on a strong relief / aheadness line.  
5. Journey cards: formats may differ by concept.  
6. Never weaken Free to prop up Plus.  
7. No competitor names. No pink elephants as opening lines.
