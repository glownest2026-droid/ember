# Conscientious Conor

**Status:** Canonical as of July 2026
**Purpose:** Quality bar for Ember product copy, Discover content, Stage 2/3 recommendations, notifications, marketplace guidance, and parent-facing UX.
**Related:** `web/docs/EMBER_BRAND_BOOK.md`, `web/docs/PRODUCT_MARKETING_LIBRARY.md`, `.cursor/rules/conscientious-conor.mdc`

---

## Who Conor Is

Conscientious Conor is a tired, thoughtful, first-time UK dad in his 30s. He works full-time, shares parenting with his partner, and has already researched the first year heavily.

His child is no longer a tiny baby. The home already has books, blocks, cups, soft toys, bath bits, relatives' gifts, and things bought in a rush. Conor does not need a generic shopping list. He needs Ember to help him see what is changing now, what to bring back out, what to ignore, and what might actually be worth buying next.

Conor will pay for Ember Plus if it saves him from long research holes and makes him feel one step ahead without being patronised or sold to.

---

## The Five Tests

Every parent-facing Ember output should pass all five.

| Test | What It Means | Fail Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Respect | Assume the parent lived the previous stage and already owns some things. | "Buy blocks", "try board books", "get a pram". |
| Well Duh | Avoid advice a thoughtful parent already knows. | Obvious activity suggestions with no age-specific shift. |
| Stage shift | Explain what is different now. | Generic claims about language, motor skills, or development. |
| Buying judgement | Help decide buy, borrow, bring back out, pre-loved, new-only, or hold off. | Catalogue-style lists without trade-offs. |
| Human parent | Sound like a smart UK parent friend. | Toy-ad hype, textbook tone, or generic AI phrasing. |

## Copy Principles

Use plain English, real moments, UK parent reality, and warm directness. Be practical about safety. Respect existing toys and gear. When in doubt, name the concrete parent decision.

Good Ember copy often says:

- "If you already have..."
- "Bring this back out when..."
- "Borrow first if..."
- "Worth buying new only if..."
- "Check the age mark / small parts / condition..."
- "This helps now because..."

Avoid:

- patronising instructions
- Americanised or clinical jargon
- overconfident developmental claims
- toy-ad excitement
- product lists without judgement
- guilt, lag, panic, or shame framing

---

## Banned Public-Copy Phrases

Do not use:

- magic
- win
- unlock
- optimise
- essential
- must-have
- developmental domain
- research shows
- research-backed
- stage-based
- proactive parenting
- accelerate development
- another spiral
- endless tabs
- 40 tabs
- six months behind

Also avoid internal prototype labels such as "Catalogue example", "Sample shortlist", and "Stage 3 card" in parent-facing copy. Use `Pip's Picks`.

## Well Duh Fixes

| Weak | Conor-Grade |
|---|---|
| Try board books. | Pause before turning the page; wait for a point, sound, or reach. |
| Go for a pram walk. | Give a familiar walk a small job: dogs, buses, three leaves. |
| Try blocks. | If you already have blocks, the shift is copying you, two-block towers, crash and repeat. |
| Buy a shape sorter. | Worth it only if they are starting to pause, turn the piece, and try again. |
| Get pretend play toys. | Add people or animals only if the play is ready for small stories, not just carrying things around. |

## Buying Judgement

Conor values Ember when it says what a product category is for and when not to buy it.

Use:

- **Buy new:** safety or hygiene matters, or condition is hard to judge.
- **Buy pre-loved:** durable, washable, easy to inspect, and often duplicated.
- **Borrow first:** common, bulky, expensive, or high ownership risk.
- **Bring back out:** likely already in the home from an earlier stage.
- **Hold off:** too early, too generic, unsafe, or likely to become clutter.

## Stage 3 Pip's Picks Bar

Pip's Picks should not feel like shopping ads. Each pick needs a job.

Every visible pick should answer:

- What is it?
- Why now?
- Who is it best for?
- What is the caveat?
- Should the family buy, borrow, buy pre-loved, or hold off?
- Is there a safety or age-mark note?
- Is this likely already owned?

If retailer links are stale, retired, blocked, or likely to mislead, use a comparison/search route and label it honestly, for example `Compare options`.

---

## Agent Duty

Before finishing work involving copy, Discover content, age-band ingestion, notifications, marketplace picks, or parent-facing UX, check the output against the five tests.

If the work fails the Conor bar, say so. Rewrite it or flag the source-data gap. Do not silently ship weak parent copy.
