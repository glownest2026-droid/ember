## Guidance Notes

- Clarity on Waterproofing: Many products market 'water-resistant covers' or 'waterproof protectors' rather than an inherently waterproof mattress. For safe sleep, the distinction between a waterproof mattress core and a waterproof cover/protector needs to be clear. Ensure our recommendations explicitly meet the 'waterproof mattress' criteria.
- Firmness Assessment: While all selected mattresses claim to be firm, the subjective nature of 'firmness' can vary. For the 1-3 month age band, a consistently firm and flat sleep surface is critical. Consider if a more objective measure or independent verification of firmness is feasible or necessary for top recommendations.
- Balancing Reviews and Expert Endorsements: Some highly-rated products by experts (e.g., Mother&Baby, Made For Mums) have fewer customer reviews. When selecting top picks, we should consider the weight given to expert awards versus a high volume of customer reviews, especially for newer or specialist products.
- Value Proposition of Dual-Sided Mattresses: Dual-sided mattresses offer extended use from baby to toddler, which is a strong selling point. However, it's crucial to ensure the 'baby side' unequivocally meets the firm and flat requirements for newborns without compromise.
- Price vs. Integrated Features: The research shows that some budget-friendly options offer integrated waterproofing, while more expensive natural options often require separate waterproof protectors. This highlights that a higher price doesn't always guarantee all desired features, and the integrated waterproofing is a key differentiator for value.
