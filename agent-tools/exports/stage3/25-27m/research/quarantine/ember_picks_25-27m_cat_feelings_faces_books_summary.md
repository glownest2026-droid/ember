# Pip’s Picks: Books about faces and feelings (25–27 months)

ownership_class: **multiple** · Top 10 / longlist 25

## Educational shift
Just past two, feelings talk shifts from mirroring a face to naming a mood with a colour, photo or short line.

## Top Ember Picks
1. **The Colour Monster** (Anna Llenas) — Best colour feelings sorter
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/441877/the-colour-monster-a-story-about-emotions-by-llenas-anna/9781787410213
   - A colour sorted feelings story that gives each mood a clear jar and picture so you can name one feeling before you turn the page.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, the shift is naming a mood with a colour cue instead of only copying a face. Stay on one colour page, wait for a point or a matching face, then turn. Start here when you want a short feelings sort on the sofa.
2. **In My Heart: A Book of Feelings** (Jo Witek) — Best heart feelings pages
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/in-my-heart-a-book-of-feelings-jo-witek/9720855
   - Die cut heart pages that give each feeling a body image so you can stay on one heart and name it before you turn.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, feelings need a body cue, not only a face on the page. Stay on one heart, wait for a word or a point, then turn. Keep the pass short so the naming stays gentle after a hard goodbye.
3. **The Feelings Book** (Todd Parr) — Best bold feelings lines
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/111060/the-feelings-book-by-parr-todd/9780316043465
   - Bold pages that name everyday feelings in plain words, best when you pick one line and match it to today before you turn.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, short feeling lines land better than long stories. Read one line, match it to something from today, wait for a nod, then turn. Keep the naming pass short and kind on the sofa after a hard goodbye.
4. **Grumpy Frog** (Ed Vere) — Best grumpy face laugh
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/1110604/grumpy-frog-by-vere-ed/9780141366128
   - A funny frog story about feeling grumpy and finding friends again, best when you pause on the grumpy face and wait for a matching try.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, grumpy needs a face and a laugh, not a scold. Pause on the grumpy page, wait for a matching face or a word, then turn. Good when colour jars are already known and you want a story face.
5. **The Colour Monster Goes to School** (Anna Llenas) — Best colour feelings at school
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/451838/the-colour-monster-goes-to-school-by-llenas-anna/9781787415263
   - A follow on colour feelings story set around school nerves, still using clear colour cues you can name one page at a time.
   - Why Pip: Once Colour Monster is familiar, school nerves need the same colour naming job. Stay on one colour page, wait for a point, then turn. Second Anna Llenas slot only when the first Colour Monster is already earning sofa time at home.
6. **The Huge Bag of Worries** (Virginia Ironside) — Best worry bag story
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/the-huge-bag-of-worries-virginia-ironside/9780340903179
   - A story about a bag of worries that gets lighter when worries are named and shared, best when you pause as the bag changes.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, worry needs a name and a share, not a long talk. Pause when the bag changes size, wait for a word or a nod, then turn so the talk stays short and kind before bed tonight at home.
7. **Happy** (Emma Dodd) — Best happy cuddle board
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/happy-emma-dodd/9781788006989
   - A short board book about what makes a little owl happy, ending in a close cuddle you can name and copy.
   - Why Pip: Some evenings only need one happy word and a cuddle, not a full feelings sort. Read the short line, name the happy cue, wait for a lean in, then close. Soft close when bigger feelings books can wait until morning.
8. **Baby Faces** (DK) — Best photo baby faces
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/baby-faces-dk/9780241188750
   - Photo faces of babies showing clear expressions, best when you match one photo to a face in the room before you turn.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, photo faces still carry the naming job on tired evenings. Match one photo to a face in the room, wait for a try, then turn. Keep this when colour jars are too much talk after a long day.
9. **Baby Touch: Faces** (Ladybird) — Best high contrast faces
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/313409/baby-touch-faces-a-black-and-white-book-by-ladybird/9780241391723
   - A sturdy Ladybird board book of large black and white faces, sized for shared looking and simple pointing on the sofa.
   - Why Pip: Faces are still the social magnet just past two when colour books get busy. High contrast keeps the look simple while you name eyes and smiles. Start here when you want a short face book before busier emotion pages later.
10. **Baby Touch First Focus: Faces** (Ladybird) — Best mirror face ending
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/289967/faces-baby-touch-first-focus-by-ladybird/9780241243251
   - A first focus faces board book with a mirror ending so they can copy one expression after looking at the pages.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, the job is matching a face on the page to a face in the room. Copy one expression in the mirror, wait for a try, then close. Second Ladybird only when Baby Touch Faces already covers high contrast looking.
