# Pip’s Picks: Bedtime books with familiar steps (25–27 months)

ownership_class: **multiple** · Top 10 / longlist 25

## Educational shift
Just past two, bedtime needs predictable steps they can join: bath, story, lights, sleep. Short familiar pages beat long new plots at the end of the day.

## Top Ember Picks
1. **Ten Minutes to Bed: Little Unicorn** (Rhiannon Fielding) — Best ten minute wind down
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/312671/ten-minutes-to-bed-little-unicorn-by-fielding-rhiannon/9780241348918
   - A countdown bedtime story with familiar wind down steps and a little unicorn racing the clock toward sleep, best when you pause for one last job before lights out.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, bedtime works better with steps they can join than a brand new plot. Count the minutes, pause for one last job, then close. Start here when you want a short wind down they already recognise from last night.
2. **Ten Minutes to Bed: Little Dinosaur** (Rhiannon Fielding) — Best dinosaur countdown bed
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/312672/ten-minutes-to-bed-little-dinosaur-by-fielding-rhiannon/9780241532676
   - The same ten minute bedtime countdown with a little dinosaur, so the familiar steps stay while the character swaps for a fresh read.
   - Why Pip: Once the unicorn countdown is known, a dinosaur swap keeps the same steps without inventing a new routine. Count the minutes, pause for one last job, then close. Second Fielding only when Little Unicorn is already the house wind down.
3. **Goodnight Moon** (Margaret Wise Brown) — Best classic goodnight round
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/108935/goodnight-moon-by-brown-margaret-wise/9781509831975
   - A classic goodnight round that names the room and says goodnight to each thing before sleep, paced for shared pointing on tired nights.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, a predictable goodnight round settles the room better than a surprise ending. Name each thing, wait for a point, then close. Keep this when the countdown books get too busy after a long day at home tonight.
4. **Guess How Much I Love You** (Sam McBratney) — Best love you bedtime closer
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/guess-how-much-i-love-you-sam-mcbratney/9781406359781
   - A short love you back and forth between Big Nutbrown Hare and Little Nutbrown Hare before sleep, ending in a soft closer.
   - Why Pip: Some nights need a love you closer more than a countdown. Trade one bigger love you, wait for a lean in, then lights out. Soft close when the countdown books already covered the wind down steps tonight at home together.
5. **Goodnight Gorilla** (Peggy Rathmann) — Best quiet zoo goodnight
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/goodnight-gorilla-peggy-rathmann/9781909263499
   - A nearly wordless goodnight zoo story where animals tip toe after the zookeeper toward bed, one stop at a time.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, a quiet goodnight chase can still work as a routine if you name each stop. Point, whisper goodnight, then turn. Useful when they want a gentle giggle without a long spoken plot before sleep tonight at home.
6. **The Going to Bed Book** (Sandra Boynton) — Best going to bed sequence
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/305833/the-going-to-bed-book-by-boynton-sandra/9780689847127
   - A short board book that walks animals through bath, brush and bed in a clear going to bed sequence you can mime together.
   - Why Pip: Bedtime just past two needs steps they can copy: bath, brush, bed. Read one step, wait for a mime, then turn. Good when countdown stories are already known and you want a plainer sequence on the sofa tonight together at home.
7. **Time for Bed** (Mem Fox) — Best soft time for bed
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/111646/time-for-bed-by-mem-fox/9780152010485
   - A gentle animal bedtime book with short lines that say it is time for bed again and again, paced for shared looking.
   - Why Pip: Some nights only need the same soft line repeated until they settle. Read one animal page, wait for a yawn cue, then turn. Soft close when the house already did bath and brush without a story fight tonight at home.
8. **On the Night You Were Born** (Nancy Tillman) — Best birth night closer
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/on-the-night-you-were-born-nancy-tillman/9780312601553
   - A lyrical night time story about the night they arrived, ending in a quiet you are loved closer for shared reading.
   - Why Pip: Some bedtimes want a you are loved closer more than a countdown. Stay on one night page, name the love cue, wait for a lean in, then lights out. Soft close after the familiar wind down steps are already done.
9. **Peace at Last** (Jill Murphy) — Best noisy house then quiet
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/peace-at-last-jill-murphy/9781509862726
   - A house that will not stay quiet while Mr Bear tries to sleep, ending when peace finally arrives after each noise.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, naming the noises that keep sleep away can settle a restless night. Pause on each noise, wait for a matching sound or a giggle, then turn toward quiet. Useful after a loud day when silence needs a story frame.
10. **Can't You Sleep, Little Bear?** (Martin Waddell) — Best bear who cannot sleep
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/can-t-you-sleep-little-bear-martin-waddell/9780744578362
   - A cave bedtime story where Big Bear helps Little Bear with the dark until sleep finally comes, page by page.
   - Why Pip: Fear of the dark often lands just past two. Stay with Big Bear as the light gets bigger, wait for a nod, then close. Soft close when the countdown books already handled the wind down and only the dark worry remains.
