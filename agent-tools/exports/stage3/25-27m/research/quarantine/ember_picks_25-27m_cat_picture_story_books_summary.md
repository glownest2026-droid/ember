# Pip’s Picks: Books that start little chats (25–27 months)

ownership_class: **multiple** · Top 10 / longlist 25

## Educational shift
Just past two, story time shifts from naming alone to short chats about what happens next and who did what.

## Top Ember Picks
1. **Dear Zoo** (Rod Campbell) — Best flap chat starter
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/108934/dear-zoo-by-campbell-rod/9780230747890
   - A sturdy lift the flap board book where each crate reveals a different animal to name, reject and send back to the zoo.
   - Why Pip: Just past two, they want to lead the page with a point and a word. Flaps that hide animals invite a short chat about too big, too fierce and just right. Start here when you want a familiar lift and talk game on the sofa.
2. **Peepo!** (Janet and Allan Ahlberg) — Best peepo story chat
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/111124/peepo-by-ahlberg-janet/9780141337425
   - A classic peepo board book following a baby through the day with holes to peep and busy home scenes to name together.
   - Why Pip: Daytime scenes give more to talk about than a single animal flap. Peep holes invite them to spot who is in the room next. Good when Dear Zoo is already known and you want home life chat on the page.
3. **Each Peach Pear Plum** (Janet and Allan Ahlberg) — Best I spy rhyme chat
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/57304/each-peach-pear-plum-by-ahlberg-janet/9780140506396
   - A rhyme and I spy picture book where storybook characters hide in busy scenes for pointing and naming together on the sofa.
   - Why Pip: I spy pages stretch chat beyond flap opens for just past two. They hunt for a face in the picture and tell you who they found today. Second Ahlberg slot when Peepo already covers hole peeping at home each day.
4. **Press Here** (Herve Tullet) — Best press and talk play
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/press-here-herve-tullet/6458474
   - An interactive picture book that asks them to press, shake and tip dots so the page seems to answer with a new pattern.
   - Why Pip: Press Here turns reading into a shared doing game with short instructions they can follow on the sofa. The chat is about what happens next after each press. Good when flaps feel old and they want a page that answers back at story time.
5. **Where's Spot?** (Eric Hill) — Best spot the dog flaps
   - https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/122792/wheres-spot-by-hill-eric/9780723263661
   - A lift the flap search for Spot behind doors and lids, with animals saying Spot is not there until the last reveal.
   - Why Pip: Search flaps keep the chat on where is he and who is behind this door. Short no answers build to a yes ending they can shout. Useful when Dear Zoo flaps are known and they want a dog hunt instead.
6. **The Very Hungry Caterpillar** (Eric Carle) — Best hungry week story
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/the-very-hungry-caterpillar-eric-carle/9780241003008
   - A classic food count story with holes through fruit pages and a caterpillar that eats through the week then turns into a butterfly.
   - Why Pip: Counting food through the week gives a clear what next chat without needing long text on the page. They can name the fruit and notice the holes together. Good when you want a short story arc they already half know from soft play visits.
7. **That's Not My Puppy** (Usborne) — Best touch and reject chat
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/that-s-not-my-puppy-fiona-watt/9780746047866
   - A touchy feely board book where each puppy is not quite right until the last soft page they can stroke and claim.
   - Why Pip: Touch patches invite a short reject and try again chat: not this one, try the next page. The ending soft patch is the yes they wait for. Good when flaps are sorted and they want texture talk on the page.
8. **Spot Goes to the Farm** (Eric Hill) — Best farm Spot flaps
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/spot-goes-to-the-farm-eric-hill/9780723263708
   - A farm visit flap book where Spot looks for animals behind barn doors and hedges for a short search and name chat.
   - Why Pip: Farm flaps extend the Spot search into animal naming on a different setting at home. Second Eric Hill only when Where is Spot is already loved each week. Skip if one Spot book is enough for your shelf right now.
9. **That's Not My Dinosaur** (Usborne) — Best dinosaur touch reject
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/that-s-not-my-dinosaur-fiona-watt/9781474916222
   - A dinosaur themed touchy feely board book with textured patches to stroke while rejecting each wrong dinosaur page until the last soft yes.
   - Why Pip: Dinosaur textures keep the reject and claim chat fresh after the puppy version at home. Second Usborne touch book only for this shelf. Useful when they already know the That is Not My pattern and want a new creature to stroke.
10. **Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See?** (Bill Martin Jr and Eric Carle) — Best colour see and say
   - https://uk.bookshop.org/p/books/brown-bear-brown-bear-what-do-you-see-bill-martin-jr/9780241135815
   - A repeating colour and animal see and say book with bold Eric Carle pictures and a line they can join in on.
   - Why Pip: Repeating lines let them join the chat before they can retell a full story alone. Colours and animals give easy points to name on each page. Good when you want a chorus style book after flap search titles on the sofa.
