# Pip's Picks: Balance paths and stepping stones (34–36 months)

## Educational shift
At nearly three, movement becomes rule-based: step, pause, turn — not random crashing. Versus 31–33m throw-and-aim games, this band suits sequenced indoor paths. Try cushions and tape before spending.

## Top 5 Ember Picks
1. **Gonge River Stones** — Best for rule-based paths. Premium reconfigurable triangles with three steepness sides. £55 Liberty House Toys.
2. **Tippi Sensory Stepping Stones (5-pack)** — Best for tight budgets. 18m+ mark clears FF for 34-month-olds; replaces National Geographic (3+ only). ~£16 Amazon UK.
3. **Edx Step-A-Stumps** — Best for small spaces. Nesting ribbed stumps + carry bag. £87.99 Dyslexia Shop.
4. **Gonge Hilltops** — Best for height practice. Three fixed heights for step-up/pause/step-down. £74.99 Liberty House Toys.
5. **Edx Step-A-Trails** — Best for straight paths. Low beam sections for heel-toe walking rules. £96 Shopedx.

## Ranked longlist summary
Backups include National Geographic 6/10-pack (strong reviews but 3+ age — backup once 36m+), Toyrific award set (sold out), Edx rope stones, Costway 9-piece, and Gonge alt stockists.

## Best substitutes if unavailable
- Gonge out → Tippi budget set, then National Geographic longlist backup (age caveat).
- Edx stumps out → Tippi or tape path hold-off.

## What we skipped and why
Stapelstein (stack not path), wobble boards, bounce stones, baby walkers, Temu unverified listings, duplicate Gonge sets.

## Borrow / bring back out / hold off guidance
Try tape and cushions first. Borrow Gonge from nursery. Pre-loved Gonge sensible — check rubber rims. Hold off if under two metres cleared floor.
