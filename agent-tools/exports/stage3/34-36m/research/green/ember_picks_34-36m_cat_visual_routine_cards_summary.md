# Pip's Picks: Visual routine cards and timers (34–36 months)

## Educational shift
At nearly three, jobs feel real — choose clothes, carry the bag, brush teeth without you narrating every step. Pictures can help when words are not enough: shoes, teeth, toilet, coat, bag. **Try photos of your actual routine first**; buy cards only if DIY visuals are not sticking after two weeks.

## Top 5 Ember Picks
1. **Visual Timetable for Children at Home** (Create Visual Aids via Amazon) — whole-day rearrangeable home symbols. £15.95 approx. Strong Amazon evidence (4.7/683).
2. **Children's Daily Routine Board** (Craftly) — morning/evening aluminium board with golden-star progress. From £24. Specialist exemption (thin reviews).
3. **Now, Next Then Visual Timetable** (Craftly) — two- or three-step transition board; tokens extra. From £11. Specialist exemption.
4. **My Communication Book for Early Years** (Create Visual Aids via Amazon) — portable book for routines that travel between carers. £25 approx. (4.5/58).
5. **Bespoke Visual Timetable** (Craftly) — custom-length metal strip; tokens extra. From £10. Eleven five-star reviews.

## What we skipped and why
- **Timers** (Time Timer, Sensory Direct): under-36 or Ages 3+ marks fail 34–36m gate; phone timer is enough for teeth.
- **ASD Now/Next boards**: strong Amazon volume but not suitable under 36 months.
- **Etsy/Temu printables**: unverified; photos-first doctrine.
- **Melissa & Doug / ChildChart**: age marks above or below band.

## Borrow / bring back out / hold off guidance
- **Photos first** — fridge pictures of real shoes, toothbrush, coat.
- **Bring back out** — simpler strips from earlier with fewer steps.
- **Hold off timers** until 36m+ when choking marks no longer overlap band.
- **Hold off full-day charts** if only one transition hurts.
