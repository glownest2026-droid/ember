## Educational shift

Friend play is still messy at nearly three, but your child can start using words, waiting a little longer and noticing when someone feels left out. Versus **28–30m** (naming feelings) and **31–33m** (worry retelling), the shift is **social repair**: sharing rows, jealous moments, sorry and turn-taking in real peer play — not another emotion chart.

### 1. Pip and Posy: The New Friend
Posy wakes to find Pip playing with someone else — jealousy, then repair without adult rescue. **Verdict:** The cleanest script for "why is my friend playing with them?" playgroup wobbles.

### 2. Can You Share, Little Whale?
Little Whale refuses to share pod, snacks and Mum's attention until friend Blue models sharing. **Verdict:** Fresh 2024 pick for snatch-and-queue rows — concrete, not abstract.

### 3. That's (Not) Mine
Two friends shout over one chair until they choose to play together. **Verdict:** Mirrors real "MINE!" playdates; short enough for tired evenings.

### 4. The Sour Grape
A grape stockpiles grudges until a birthday mix-up teaches forgiveness. **Verdict:** Funny route into sorry and moving on after friend rows cool down.

### 5. The Squirrels Who Squabbled
Two squirrels race for the last nut until teamwork saves them. **Verdict:** Rhyming chase for when neither child will take turns.
