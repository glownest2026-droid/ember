## Educational shift

At nearly three, movement becomes **planned and rule-based** — step, pause, turn, jump — not daredevil crashing. Versus **31–33m hoops and beanbags** (aim, throw, jump-over markers), this band is about **sequenced balance paths** and indoor obstacle courses. Ownership note stands: **try cushions and tape first**; buy when a sturdier setup would help.

### 1. Gonge River Stones — Best for rule-based paths
Six triangular stones with **three steepness sides** each — lay a path, call the rules (pause on blue, turn, jump). OT/clinic staple; stacks away. **Verdict:** Worth it if tape-and-cushions has run out of steam; borrow or pre-loved first at this price.

### 2. National Geographic 6-pack — Best for tight budgets
Flat stones + **colour spinner** for rule games. Good Play Guide tested. **Verdict:** Best first buy without Gonge money; less height than premium stones.

### 3. Edx Step-A-Stumps — Best for small spaces
**Nesting stumps** with ribbed sensory tops and carry bag. **Verdict:** Flat dwellers who want tidy storage and textured feedback under feet.

### 4. Gonge Hilltops — Best for height practice
Three **fixed heights** for step-up, pause, step-down — garden or cleared room. **Verdict:** Height sequencing, not bounce thrills; pair with River Stones only if family wants the full kit.

### 5. Edx Step-A-Trails — Best for straight paths
Six **low balance-beam sections** — walk the line, pause, turn. **Verdict:** The “path” half of the category; for cautious walkers before big jumps.
