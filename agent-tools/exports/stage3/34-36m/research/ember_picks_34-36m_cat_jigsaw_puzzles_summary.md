# Pip's Picks: First jigsaw puzzles (34–36 months)

**Research date:** 2026-07-21 · **Researcher:** cursor · **Market:** UK

## Educational shift

At nearly three, puzzles stop being about posting a peg and start being about a small plan: turn the piece, compare it to the picture, try another slot. This band needs **real interlocking jigsaws** (roughly 3–12 pieces) with the **full picture visible** while they work — not baby peg boards they mastered at 28–30m.

- **Peg/knob puzzles** (Melissa & Doug jumbo knob, Bigjigs peg farm) — wrong mechanism; bring back out, don't rebuy.
- **Orchard Farmyard 6×2-piece** — 18m two-piece set; too babyish for a fresh nearly-three buy.
- **50-piece Orchard Big Dinosaurs** — beyond this card's piece-count band.
- **Giant Road floor puzzle** — road-layout play, not single-picture strategy jigsaws.

## Borrow / bring back out / hold off guidance

- **Borrow/pre-loved:** Puzzle sets travel well — count pieces before paying.
- **Bring back out:** Peg puzzles and any 2-piece sets already owned; use before buying duplicates.
- **Hold off:** 20+ floor puzzles and 50-piece character sets until 12-piece feels easy.
