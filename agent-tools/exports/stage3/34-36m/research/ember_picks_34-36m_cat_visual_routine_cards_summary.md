# Pip's Picks: Visual routine cards and timers (34–36 months)

**Research date:** 2026-07-21 · **Researcher:** cursor · **Market:** UK  
**Cluster:** Real jobs, routines and self-care · **Buyer mode:** Parent buy (not gift)

## Educational shift

At nearly three, little jobs feel **real** — choose clothes, find shoes, brush teeth, carry the bag. Words alone often bounce off mid-transition. **Pictures first:** photos of your actual routine may beat generic cards. Buy cards or timers only when DIY visuals are not sticking.

Versus **31–33m**, the shift is whole-job pride, not just copying a single step.

## What we skipped and why

- **Etsy generic printables** — inconsistent quality; photos first  
- **SoftSignals night tags** — bedtime-only, too narrow  
- **Twinkl printables** — subscription prints vs free phone photos  
- **Temu unbranded sets** — unverified safety  
- **60-minute sand timer** — wrong duration for teeth/dressing jobs

## Borrow / bring back out / hold off guidance

1. **Photos first** — real routine on fridge before any purchase  
2. **Bring back out** simpler 22–24m picture strips with fewer steps  
3. **Borrow** Now/Next boards from nursery where possible  
4. **Hold off** on full-day timetables if one transition is the only pain point
