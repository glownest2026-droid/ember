## Educational shift

At nearly three, your child is past naming things in books — they want to **ask why, explain, predict and lead the page**. This band needs **choice-led and call-and-response** picture books, not baby board books (28–30m) or simple story-arc follow-alongs (31–33m).

## What we skipped and why

- **The Gruffalo** — likely owned; passive narrative, not question-led
- **Dear Zoo** — baby flap board book; naming only
- **Press Here** — 28–30m cause-and-effect, not explaining why
- **There's a Monster in Your Book** — 28–30m tactile interactive
- **Little Dinosaurs, Big Questions** — KS1+ length and philosophy
- **We're Going on a Bear Hunt** — chant-along classic; bring back out
- **The Very Hungry Caterpillar (board book)** — baby board book

## Borrow / bring back out / hold off guidance

- **Borrow first** on You Choose, Would You Rather and Pigeon — many families already own one of these.
- **Bring back out** 28–30m naming books for vocabulary, but add one fresh choice-led pick (Twenty Questions or Tell Your Own Story).
- **Hold off** buying Gruffalo, Dear Zoo or Bear Hunt as "new" if they are already on the shelf.
- **Daytime vs bedtime:** Twenty Questions and Pigeon buzz brains — better for sofa chats than sleepy bedtime.
