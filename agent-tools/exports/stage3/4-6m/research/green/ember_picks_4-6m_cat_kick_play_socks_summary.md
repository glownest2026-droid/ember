# Pip’s Picks: Kick-and-discover socks (4–6 months)

## Educational shift
At 4–6m kicks become purposeful experiments, not newborn flails.

## Top 5 Ember Picks
1. **Lamaze Gardenbug Foot Finder and Wrist Rattle Set** — Best for classic foot finders
   - A Lamaze Gardenbug set with soft classic foot finder socks and Velcro wrist rattles that jingle when legs and arms move during supervised floor play.
   - Why Pip: Kicks start making noise on purpose at this age. Soft bug socks stay put so a flail becomes a rattle without needing a precise grab yet. Use on the playmat during nappy changes when feet are already in the air and you can watch the whole time.
2. **Playgro Jungle Friends Gift Pack** — Best for foot and wrist packs
   - A Playgro Jungle Friends pack with zebra and giraffe wrist rattles, soft foot finders and linking teethers for kick and reach rewards on the mat.
   - Why Pip: If Gardenbug is already in the drawer, this pack still earns a look for extra foot finders and chew links. Kicks that rattle teach action and sound without needing a grab first. Rotate pieces when one is drying after a wash cycle.
3. **Playgro Wrist Rattle and Foot Fingers** — Best for bright foot rattles
   - A Playgro gift box of sunny wrist and foot rattles designed to reward arm and leg movement during supervised floor play on the mat.
   - Why Pip: A brighter alternative to character jungle packs if you want plain action and sound on wrists and feet. Movement makes the noise, which is the whole job at this age. Stay with supervised awake play; never leave rattles on for sleep or unsupervised spells.
4. **Sophie La Girafe Original Teether** — Best for mouthing after kicks
   - The original Sophie La Girafe natural rubber teether kept beside kick socks so when feet and hands meet the mouth, a soft rubber chew is ready on the mat.
   - Why Pip: Kicking discovery often ends with toes or toys in the mouth. Sophie covers that next beat when socks have done their noisy job. Ranked after true foot finders because dedicated socks are the category spine, while Sophie is the mouthing companion on the same mat.
5. **Lamaze Jacques the Peacock** — Best for hanging kick toys
   - A soft Lamaze Jacques Peacock with hanging bits and a clip, so you can hang a colourful kick target above the playmat during supervised floor play.
   - Why Pip: When socks are washing, a hanging peacock still rewards a kick or bat with movement and sound. Clip it to a gym bar or pram frame where feet can reach. Ranked after true foot finders because socks teach body mapping more directly than a hanging toy alone.
