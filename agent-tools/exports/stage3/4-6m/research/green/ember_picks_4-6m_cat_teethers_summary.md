# Pip’s Picks: Teethers and chew-safe toys (4–6 months)

## Educational shift
At 4–6m gums get busy; everything travels to the mouth with more force.

## Top 5 Ember Picks
1. **Sophie La Girafe Original Teether** — Best for classic natural rubber
   - The original Sophie La Girafe natural rubber teether with soft chewable limbs and a gentle squeak, presented for early gumming when everything starts heading to the mouth.
   - Why Pip: Mouthing ramps up hard at four to six months as gums get busy. Soft Sophie legs reach sore spots without hard plastic corners. Wipe clean only, and expect her to live in the changing bag. Keep sessions short and stay within arm reach on the floor.
2. **Matchstick Monkey Original Teether** — Best for silicone monkey teethers
   - A Matchstick Monkey silicone teether with easy grip arms and textured bumps for gums, dishwasher and steriliser friendly for the heavy mouthing stretch at this age.
   - Why Pip: Parents who want dishwasher friendly teethers often bounce off Sophie wipe only rules. Matchstick Monkey bumps massage gums and the arms stay grabable when fists are still clumsy. Chill in the fridge if that helps your baby on a sore day.
3. **Sophie La Girafe Multi-Textured Teether** — Best for textured Sophie rings
   - A Sophie So Pure teething ring with sliding shapes and varied chew surfaces for babies who prefer a ring over a giraffe body when gums flare.
   - Why Pip: Not every baby loves the classic giraffe shape. This ring keeps Sophie rubber feel with more textures to work along the gum line. Treat it as Sophie number two, not Sophie number three in the same changing bag basket this month.
4. **Playgro Jungle Friends Gift Pack** — Best for several chew pieces
   - A Playgro Jungle Friends pack that includes three textured linking teethers alongside wrist and foot pieces, so you can rotate chews when one is in the wash.
   - Why Pip: Teething weeks mean lost teethers under sofas. A pack with several chew links keeps a clean one ready without another emergency shop run. Start with the linking teethers on the mat before adding the soft foot pieces if hands are the main job today.
5. **Lamaze Flip Flap Dragon Rattle** — Best for crinkle chew toys
   - A Lamaze Flip Flap Dragon soft rattle with crinkly feet and a clip that flaps the wings, giving a mouthable soft toy when gums want fabric as well as rubber.
   - Why Pip: Some babies chew fabric as hard as silicone. Flip Flap offers crinkle feet and a soft body when rubber teethers are rejected for an hour. Keep a proper silicone or rubber teether as the main gum tool beside it on the mat.
