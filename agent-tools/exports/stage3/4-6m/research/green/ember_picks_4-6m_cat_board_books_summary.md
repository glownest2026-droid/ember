# Pip’s Picks: Chunky board books (4–6 months)

## Educational shift
At 4–6m looking lasts longer and babble joins the page; books become a chat tool, not only a high-contrast flash.

## Top 10 Ember Picks
1. **Dear Zoo Lift the Flap 40th Anniversary Edition** — Best for flap animal chat
   - A sturdy Dear Zoo board book with big animal flaps to lift, sized for shared looking when babble and pointing start to join the page on the sofa.
   - Why Pip: Talk stretches past coos now, and animal flaps give a clear pause plus a reveal to name together. Keep turns short on the sofa so the book stays a chat, not a chew marathon. Stop while the look still feels friendly and close the covers.
2. **Where's Spot? (Board Book)** — Best for spot the dog flaps
   - Eric Hill Where is Spot board book with simple yellow flaps and a familiar dog hunt, easy for a parent to hold while a baby watches and waits.
   - Why Pip: Waiting for Spot under a flap matches the new patience for a short reveal. One or two flaps per sitting is enough; celebrate the look, then close the book while it still feels friendly. Stay close on the sofa and keep the hunt short.
3. **Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See?** — Best for colour animal chorus
   - Bill Martin and Eric Carle Brown Bear board book with bold colour animals and a repeating question rhythm parents can chant without reading every word aloud.
   - Why Pip: A repeating question gives tired parents a ready refrain when freestyle chat runs dry. Bright animals hold a longer look than newborn high contrast alone. Keep one animal per turn, then close the book while the look still feels friendly together.
4. **Peekaboo Bear** — Best for slider peekaboo
   - A Nosy Crow Peekaboo Bear board book with chunky sliders and a mirror ending, built for short peekaboo rounds on a lap during awake play.
   - Why Pip: Peekaboo is the game of the season, and sliders make the hide and find physical without needing tiny finger control yet. Stop after a few slides before frustration. Stay close on the lap and close the book while smiles are still easy.
5. **That's Not My Puppy...** — Best for textured puppy pages
   - Usborne That is Not My Puppy board book with soft patches and a mouse to spot on each spread, on sturdy board pages for shared looking.
   - Why Pip: Hands explore while ears hear the same refrain. Soft textures give a second job besides looking, useful when mouthing is the default. One patch per sitting is enough; close the book while the look still feels friendly on the sofa.
6. **That's Not My Cat...** — Best for textured cat pages
   - Usborne That is Not My Cat board book with bold textured patches to stroke and the same simple refrain families already know from the series.
   - Why Pip: If Puppy already lives in the basket, Cat still works as a second texture set without a new story pattern to learn. Rotate when one is sticky with milk. Keep turns short and stop while the shared look still feels friendly.
7. **Baby Touch: Faces** — Best for bold face pages
   - A Ladybird Baby Touch Faces board book of large friendly faces on sturdy pages, still useful when looking stretches past newborn high contrast alone.
   - Why Pip: Faces stay the favourite subject even as colour arrives. Hold it close during a feed break so a chat can ride on a familiar look. One or two pages is enough; stop while the shared look still feels friendly and put the book away.
8. **Baby Touch: Animals** — Best for bold animal pages
   - A Ladybird Baby Touch Animals board book with clear animal shapes in bold tones for early shared looking and naming on sturdy board pages.
   - Why Pip: Animals give a second bold lane when faces feel done for the day. Keep pages slow: one animal, one sound, then pause. Close the book while the look still feels friendly rather than racing through every spread in one sitting.
9. **Hairy Maclary and Zachary Quack** — Best for bounce along rhyme
   - Lynley Dodd Hairy Maclary and Zachary Quack board book with bouncing rhyme and animal friends to point out together on sturdy pages.
   - Why Pip: Rhythm carries a longer listen than a single picture page. Use it when they are settled on your chest and ready for a short story pulse. Keep one verse, then stop while the listen still feels friendly rather than pushing a full chapter.
10. **Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes** — Best for song body pages
   - A Childs Play Head Shoulders Knees and Toes board book that pairs the familiar song with body pictures for pointing and gentle touch.
   - Why Pip: Song plus page makes body words stick without a talking to. Touch their knees while you sing; the book is the prompt, not homework. Keep one verse, then stop while smiles are still easy and put the book back on the shelf.
