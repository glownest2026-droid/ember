# Pip’s Picks: Reach-and-grab toys (4–6 months)

## Educational shift
At 4–6m grabs become purposeful; need bigger targets than newborn brushes.

## Top 5 Ember Picks
1. **Lamaze Freddie The Firefly Soft Toy** — Best for several grab points
   - A soft Lamaze Freddie Firefly with layered wings, a peekaboo mirror, clinking rings and a tethered ladybird teether, plus a clip for the pram or playmat edge.
   - Why Pip: Hands are aiming more now, not just brushing past toys. Freddie gives several places to grab and something to mouth once the hold sticks. Clip him where they can see him during floor play so a reach has a clear target within arm reach on the mat.
2. **Lamaze Captain Calamari** — Best for soft octopus grabs
   - A soft Lamaze Captain Calamari octopus with long grabable legs and mixed textures, sized so both hands can catch hold during supervised floor or pram play.
   - Why Pip: Single hand brushes are turning into proper holds. Long soft legs give a bigger target when aim is still wobbly. Keep play on the mat so a triumphant grab does not end under the sofa cushions again, and stay within arm reach the whole time.
3. **Sophie La Girafe Original Teether** — Best for rubber giraffe grabs
   - The original Sophie La Girafe natural rubber teether with soft limbs and a gentle squeak, light enough for small hands to lift toward the mouth during floor play.
   - Why Pip: Reaching often ends in the mouth at this age. Soft Sophie legs give something to catch and chew without hard plastic edges. Wipe clean only; she is not dishwasher gear for the steriliser cycle. Keep sessions short and stay within arm reach on the floor.
4. **Sophie La Girafe Multi-Textured Teether** — Best for textured ring grabs
   - A Sophie La Girafe So Pure teething ring with sliding geometric shapes and varied chew surfaces, light for small hands to lift and mouth on the playmat.
   - Why Pip: Some babies prefer a ring they can slide and chew over a giraffe body. The textures reward a successful grab with something interesting on the gums. Keep this as the second Sophie shape, not a third Sophie buy in the same basket.
5. **Playgro Jungle Friends Gift Pack** — Best for linking grab packs
   - A Playgro Jungle Friends gift pack with wrist rattles, soft foot finders and three textured linking teethers, giving several grabable pieces for supervised floor play.
   - Why Pip: A single toy can bore a determined grabber. This pack spreads reaches across links, wrists and feet without buying five separate pieces. Use the linking teethers on the mat first if the soft foot pieces feel fiddly at four months old.
